# Disneyland Paris: The Complete 2025 Guide

**Disneyland Paris brings Disney magic to Europe** with two parks, world-class attractions, and a distinct European flair. Home to arguably the most beautiful Sleeping Beauty Castle and the new Avengers Campus, it's the most visited theme park in Europe.

## The Two Parks

### Disneyland Park
The original park with classic Disney attractions and Sleeping Beauty Castle.

### Walt Disney Studios Park
Film-focused park featuring Avengers Campus, Ratatouille, and more thrill rides.

## Must-Do Attractions

### Disneyland Park

**Big Thunder Mountain**
- Arguable best version worldwide
- Island location with excellent theming
- Height Requirement: 102cm
- Wait times: 30-75 minutes

**Phantom Manor**
- Unique Western Haunted Mansion
- Darker story than US versions
- Different experience entirely
- Wait times: 15-45 minutes

**Pirates of the Caribbean**
- Longer and more elaborate than WDW
- Audio-animatronic Jack Sparrow
- Wait times: 20-45 minutes

**Hyperspace Mountain**
- Star Wars themed Space Mountain
- Inversions! (unlike US versions)
- Height Requirement: 120cm
- Wait times: 30-60 minutes

### Walt Disney Studios Park

**Avengers Campus (2022)**
- Spider-Man W.E.B. Adventure
- Avengers Assemble: Flight Force
- Meet Marvel characters
- Stunning Quinjet theming

**Ratatouille: L'Aventure**
- Trackless dark ride
- Shrink to rat size
- Incredible theming
- Height: None

**Crush's Coaster**
- Spinning shell coaster
- Finding Nemo themed
- Extremely popular
- Height: 107cm

**Tower of Terror**
- Twilight Zone theming
- Multiple drop sequences
- Height: 102cm

## Best Time to Visit

### Crowd Calendar
- **Lowest:** Mid-January to mid-March (excluding half-terms), November (except Halloween)
- **Moderate:** April, May, September, October
- **Busiest:** Summer holidays, Christmas, Halloween, Easter

### French/UK School Holidays
French and British holidays heavily impact crowds:
- **February half-term** — Very busy
- **Easter** — Extremely busy
- **Summer (July-August)** — Peak crowds
- **Halloween/Christmas** — Special events, very busy

### Operating Hours
- **Low Season:** 10 AM - 8 PM
- **Peak Season:** 9 AM - 11 PM
- **Extra Magic Time:** 8:30 AM for hotel guests

## Getting There

### By Train
- **Eurostar** from London: 2h45 to Marne-la-Vallée station (5 min walk to parks)
- **TGV** from Paris: 40 min from Paris CDG or city

### By Plane
- **Paris CDG Airport:** 45 min by bus/train
- **Paris Orly:** 60 min by bus

### By Car
- 32 km east of Paris
- A4 motorway
- Parking: €35/day

## Insider Tips

### Beat the Crowds
1. **Extra Magic Time** — Book Disney hotel for early entry
2. **Rope Drop Strategy** — Studios Park typically has worse waits
3. **Single Rider** — Available on Crush's Coaster, others
4. **Premier Access** — Worth it for Avengers Campus

### What Americans Should Know
- **Smoking** more prevalent than US parks
- **Service style** different — be patient
- **Food quality** generally better than US Disney
- **Merchandise** includes European exclusives

### Weather Prep
- **Rain** is common year-round — bring layers
- **Winter** can be cold — park is still magical
- **Summer** rarely extreme heat

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many days do I need?
Two days for both parks comfortably. Three days for a relaxed pace. One day is rushed but possible for highlights.

### Is it worth it compared to US Disney parks?
Yes, for different reasons. Unique attractions (Phantom Manor, Crush's Coaster), beautiful castle, Avengers Campus, and more adult-friendly atmosphere (alcohol, dining). Some attractions are better versions than US.

### Which park has longer waits?
Walt Disney Studios due to fewer attractions. Premier Access most valuable here.

### Is the Eurostar worth it?
From London, absolutely. City center to park entrance in under 3 hours with no airport hassle.

Disneyland Paris proves Disney magic translates beautifully to Europe. With unique attractions, stunning aesthetics, and that distinct French touch, it's an essential Disney destination.
